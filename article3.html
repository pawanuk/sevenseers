Beyond the Plausible CV: Why We Use 16PF Profiling for C-Level Hires

A CV tells you what a candidate has done. A competency assessment tells you how they did it, and whether they can do it again in your specific corporate culture.

At the C-suite level, almost every candidate who makes it to the final shortlist has a highly plausible CV. They have the right degrees from the right institutions. They have held the right titles at the right companies. They know how to interview well, and they know exactly what a board wants to hear.

Yet, executive failure rates remain stubbornly high. When a senior leader fails, it is almost never because they lacked technical competence or industry knowledge. They fail because of behavioural misalignment.

The Limits of the Interview

Traditional unstructured interviews are notoriously poor predictors of future performance. An articulate candidate can easily mask underlying behavioural risks during a series of one-hour conversations. They can claim to be highly collaborative while actually being deeply autocratic. They can claim to be adaptable while actually being rigidly dogmatic.

To mitigate this risk, SevenSeers integrates formal psychometric profiling into the executive search process, specifically utilising the 16PF (Sixteen Personality Factor) Questionnaire.

Why 16PF?

Unlike popular typing tools (like Myers-Briggs or DiSC) which sort people into broad, unnuanced categories, the 16PF is a robust, trait-based instrument backed by decades of empirical research. It measures 16 primary personality factors — such as emotional stability, dominance, rule-consciousness, and openness to change — on a continuous scale.

As a Certified 16PF Practitioner, Phaniraj Bhatt does not just hand over a computer-generated report. The psychometric data is mapped directly against the specific behavioural competencies required for your mandate.

Predicting Cultural Fit

Consider a mandate for a VP of Engineering at a rapidly scaling start-up. The candidate has a stellar track record at a massive, process-heavy multinational. The interview goes perfectly.

However, the 16PF profile reveals extreme high scores in ‘Rule-Consciousness’ and ‘Perfectionism’, combined with very low scores in ‘Openness to Change’. This data strongly predicts that the candidate will struggle in the chaotic, pivot-heavy environment of a start-up. They will try to impose rigid multinational processes on a team that needs agility.

This insight allows the hiring committee to probe these specific areas during final interviews, or to make an informed decision to pass on a candidate who looked perfect on paper.

Executive search is not about finding someone who can do the job. It is about finding someone who will thrive doing the job in your specific environment. Formal assessment is the bridge between a plausible CV and a successful hire.
