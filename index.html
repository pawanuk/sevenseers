How it runs

Five steps, and you speak to me at each of them.

  1. Brief

    A conversation about the role, the reporting line, and what the last person got wrong. Confidentiality terms agreed here.

  2. Market map

    The companies worth approaching, the people inside them, and an honest read on what the role will cost.

  3. Approach

    I make direct, discreet contact with passive candidates. Your name is disclosed only when you agree it should be.

  4. Assessment

    A short list, each name with a competency‑based report behind it. 16PF profiling where the role warrants it.

  5. Offer & landing

    Compensation benchmarking, reference checks, offer negotiation — and I stay in contact through the notice period, so the hire actually arrives.

Discretion

Most senior mandates are confidential. That is normal.

If you are replacing an incumbent, restructuring quietly, or hiring ahead of an announcement, the search runs without public advertisement. Your identity is disclosed to candidates only when you agree it should be, and everything shared in a mandate — CVs, references, conversations — stays within it.

Candidates I place are not approached again for other mandates, and I do not source from a client while an engagement is live.

One more thing, since it is usually the unasked question: large search firms operate on volume. A senior partner pitches to win your mandate, and the actual search is handed down to junior researchers. When you brief me, the person you brief is the person who maps the market, makes the approach, runs the assessment, and negotiates the offer. That is how a mandate gets closed in one month on a single CV.
