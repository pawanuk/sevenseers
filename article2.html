Executive Search in the Age of Scarcity: The 3% Rule

Less than 3% of senior engineering leaders in India are actively looking for a new role. If your search strategy relies on inbound applications or job boards, you are only seeing the desperate, not the capable.

There is a fundamental misunderstanding about the engineering talent market in India. The sheer volume of engineering graduates produced annually creates an illusion of abundance. But at the leadership level — particularly in deep-tech domains like power electronics, fuel cell technology, or aerospace structural mechanics — there is acute, crippling scarcity.

The Illusion of the Database

When a client retains a large, volume-driven recruitment agency, they are essentially paying for access to a database. The agency runs a keyword search, sends out a mass email, and forwards the CVs of whoever replies.

This approach captures the active market. These are candidates who are unhappy, underperforming, or currently unemployed. In a highly specialised technical field, the best people are never in the active market. They are heavily compensated, deeply engaged in their current projects, and fiercely protected by their employers.

The 3% Reality

Our market mapping consistently shows that in any given niche — say, CFD experts with global team leadership experience — only about 3% of the qualified talent pool is actively looking to move. The other 97% are passive.

If you want to hire from the 97%, you cannot rely on an advertisement. You cannot rely on a junior researcher sending LinkedIn messages.

The Anatomy of a Passive Approach

Approaching a passive, highly successful engineering leader requires credibility. When they receive a call, they are not looking for a job; they are looking for a reason to hang up.

To keep them on the phone, the person calling must understand the technical nuances of their current work. They must be able to articulate exactly why the new mandate represents a genuine intellectual or career elevation, not just a lateral move for slightly more money.

This is why SevenSeers operates exclusively on a principal-led model. When we map a market, we identify the exact 15 people in the country who can do the job. Phaniraj Bhatt personally approaches each one. Because he understands the engineering context, he can have a peer-level conversation that a junior recruiter simply cannot.

The result is access to the 97% of the market that your competitors never even see. In an age of scarcity, that is the only competitive advantage that matters.
